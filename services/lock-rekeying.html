What Is Lock Rekeying?

A Smarter Way to Secure Your Home

Lock rekeying involves changing the internal pin configuration of your existing lock cylinder so that old keys no longer work. New keys are then cut to match the updated pins. The lock hardware stays in place — only the internal mechanics change.

It's a fantastic option when your lock is in good condition but you need to ensure previous keyholders can no longer access your property. It's typically more affordable than full lock replacement and just as effective for security purposes.

When Should You Rekey Your Locks?

  • Moving into a new home — you don't know who has copies of the old keys
  • Lost or stolen keys — make the missing keys useless immediately
  • After a relationship breakdown — regain control of who has access
  • Landlords between tenants — cost-effective way to change access
  • Staff turnover at businesses — revoke access without replacing hardware
